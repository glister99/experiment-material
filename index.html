1. What is the project’s purpose?
There is a growing trend for instructional videos, especially under the current circumstances. People can watch these videos to learn many things: from learning how to cook a meal, to learning for school/university. In this research project we are interested in understanding how to design instructional videos to better support learning.

2. Why have I been chosen?
For this study we have recruited participants who are over the age of 18, and are residents of the United Kingdom.

3. Do I have to take part?
It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you do decide to take part, you will be emailed this information sheet to keep and be asked to sign a consent form. You can withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without it affecting any benefits that you are entitled to. If you decide to withdraw, you will be asked what you wish to happen to the data you have provided up to that point.

4. What will happen to me if I take part?
Your involvement in the research will take no more than 40 minutes of your time. You will be asked to watch two different versions of a same 10-minute lecture video on this website, after each watching session you will be invited to answer a questionnaire. When a video is playing, your window/tab switching activities will be logged automatically, in an anonymized fashion.

5. Will I be recorded and how will the recorded media be used?
The anonymized log of your activities will be used only for analysis. No other use will be made of them without your written permission, and no one outside the project will be allowed access to the original log.

6. What are the possible benefits of taking part?
It is hoped that this work will help us to learn how to make better instructional videos so that people can learn new tasks easier from instructional videos. Additionally, if the data you provided passes our quality check, you will receive £7 through Prolific.

7. What if something goes wrong?
If you wish to make a complaint regarding your treatment by the researcher who is conducting this study please contact Duncan Brumby ([removed]). Should you feel that your complaint has not been handled satisfactorily please contact the Chair of the UCL Interaction Centre Ethics Officer ([removed]).

8. Will my taking part in this project be kept confidential?
All the information that we collect about you during the course of the research will be kept strictly confidential. You will not be able to be identified in any ensuing reports or publications.

9. Limits to confidentiality
Confidentiality may be limited and conditional and the researcher has a duty of care to report to the relevant authorities possible harm/danger to the participant or others.

10. What will happen to the results of the research project?
This research is being conducted as part of an MSc Project and the results will be presented in a MSc dissertation. Student dissertations will be read by examiners and may also be made publicly available (i.e., published on a website by the student or by the university). The results of this work might also contribute to published research articles. All the information that we collect about you during the course of the research will be kept strictly confidential. You will not be able to be identified in any ensuing reports or publications.

Immersive Experience Questionnaire

Please answer this questionnaire with the video you have just watched in mind.

1. To what extent did the video hold your attention?


2. To what extent did you feel you were focused on the instructional video?

3. How much effort did you put into watching the video?

4. Did you feel that you were trying your best to follow the events of the video?

5. To what extent did you feel consciously aware of being in the real world whilst watching?

6. To what extent were you aware of yourself in your surroundings?

7. To what extent did you notice events taking place around you?

8. To what extent could you picture yourself in the scene of the events shown in the video?

9. To what extent did you feel like you were separated from your real-world environment?

10. To what extent did you feel that the video was something you were experiencing, rather than something you were just watching?

11. To what extent was your sense of being in the environment shown in the video stronger than your sense of being in the real world?

12. To what extent did you find the concepts and themes of the video challenging?

13. To what extent did you feel motivated to keep on watching?

14. To what extent did you find the concepts and themes easy to understand?

15. To what extent did you feel like you were making progress towards understanding what was happening, and what you thought might happen at the end?

16. How well do you think you understood what happened?

17. To what extent were you interested in seeing how the events in the video would progress?

18. How much did you want the events in the video to unfold successfully for the main characters involved?

19. Were you in suspense about how the events would unfold?

20. At any point did you find yourself become so involved that you wanted to speak to the video directly?

21. To what extent did you enjoy the cinematography, graphics and/or imagery?

22. How much would you say you enjoyed watching the video?

23. When it was over, were you disappointed that you had to stop watching?

24. Would you like to watch more of this, or similar content, in the future?
